                                  The Time When I was Bullied

It all started when I walked into to school on a Friday morning. “It’s going to be a beautiful day.” I thought as I walked in the school. The birds were chirping the sun was shining  and the wind sang as I walked in the breeze.  On this day I was going to be dancing at the pep rally for spirit week. It was October and I was still new at being a 9th grader and I knew I was going to be even more nervous because I was going to be performing in front of the whole entire school. I didn't really know everyone yet and I could feel the sweat dripping down my body. “As I walked in the school, a girl asked me “Is that really how you have your hair styled?”I replied “Yes” in an annoyed tone. I was annoyed because I didn't like how she came at me. At that point, I felt as hot as a boiling pot because I was very upset. In my mind I wanted to fight her but I realized that wasn't the right thing to do and I decided to be the bigger person. “Slam!!” went the door as I put my things into my locker. I decided to look down at my arm and I spotted my Gemini bracelet glowing in my eyesight. I knew that the bracelet was going to help me through my day. I started to calm down a little bit and I went through my classes and I felt ok. Then later on at the start of my lunch period, I start to get really dirty and nasty looks in the hallway as I was making my way  to get my lunch. I also assume people were talking about me. At that moment I decided to turn back and walk the other way because I didn't feel like I was in the mood to eat anything. I didn’t like it when people would  point and stare at me.  Deep down inside I was still insecure about my hair and at that point I wasn't liking the fact that I was different and I stood out from everyone else. I came home from school and I start talking to my mom and I told her what happened. She decides to help me and tells me that I have to be proud of my hair and be happy with the hair God blessed me with. She also tells me that your hair is you don't let anyone change it or talk bad about it. She then told me not to be like everyone else and that I always had to be myself and stay true to myself. Some of my friends tell me that I don’t need to worry about what people have to say about you and continue with your life. I then started to realize that “Hey it’s my hair not theirs.” and I started to feel comfortable about my hair. People started to shower me with compliments about my hair. I began to embrace my natural hair and I learned that it was beautiful. One last thing, your hair is you; don’t let anyone tell you how you should style it.
